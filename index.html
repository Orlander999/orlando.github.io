How does an aircraft generate lift?

According to Bernoulli's principle, the curved upper surface of the wing accelerates airflow, creating lower pressure compared to the flat lower surface. This pressure difference generates lift, enabling flight. Aircraft control surfaces (ailerons, elevators, rudders) adjust aerodynamic forces to change attitude and trajectory.

How do spacecraft navigate without GPS?

Inertial navigation systems use gyroscopes and accelerometers to measure acceleration/angular velocity. Integration of these data provides velocity/position updates autonomously, though errors accumulate over time.
